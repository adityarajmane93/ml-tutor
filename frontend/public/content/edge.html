1. Intuision: What is a Pipeline in the AI Project Cycle?

Every AI project follows a sequence of steps known as the 𝗔𝗜 𝗣𝗿𝗼𝗷𝗲𝗰𝘁 𝗖𝘆𝗰𝗹𝗲, which flows from Data Acquisition, to Data Exploration and Processing, to Modeling, and finally to Evaluation.

The arrows in your workspace physically represent moving from one stage of this cycle to the next. In computer science, a series of connected data processing steps is called a 𝗣𝗶𝗽𝗲𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗲.

An arrow represents the 𝗳𝗹𝗼𝘄 𝗼𝗳 𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗮. It carries the information from the output of one node directly into the input of the next.

2. Context of ML-Tutor

Look at the black arrows connecting the nodes in your workspace. There is a strict, logical sequence you must follow to build a working pipeline:

  • 𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮𝘀𝗲𝘁 𝘁𝗼 𝗣𝗿𝗲𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗰𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴: Drag an arrow from the right side of the blue 𝗗𝗮𝘁𝗮𝘀𝗲𝘁 node to the left side of the orange 𝗣𝗿𝗲𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗰𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴 node. This pushes your raw data into the cleaning phase.
  • 𝗣𝗿𝗲𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗰𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘁𝗼 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹: Drag an arrow from the orange 𝗣𝗿𝗲𝗽𝗿𝗼𝗰𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴 node to the red-colored 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹 node. This ensures your algorithm only receives clean, formatted data.
  • 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹 𝘁𝗼 𝗘𝘃𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻: Drag an arrow from the red 𝗠𝗼𝗱𝗲𝗹 node to the green 𝗘𝘃𝗮𝗹𝘂𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 node. This passes the machine's final predictions over to be graded.

𝗔𝗱𝘃𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲𝗱 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝗻𝗲𝗰𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻𝘀 (𝗕𝗿𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗵𝗶𝗻𝗴): Did you know data can flow in multiple directions? Sometimes, you might need to clean your data using more than one strategy. In ML-Tutor, you can drag multiple arrows out of one node! For example, you can drag one arrow from your Dataset into a Preprocessing node that uses "Fill Missing Values", and a second arrow from that same Dataset into another Preprocessing node that uses "Remove Rows". This is called creating a 𝗦𝘆𝘀𝘁𝗲𝗺 𝗠𝗮𝗽 of complex relationships.

"Run Pipeline" & Troubleshooting

Once your arrows are all connected from left to right, you have built a complete map for the computer to follow.

  • 𝗥𝘂𝗻𝗻𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗔𝗜: At the top right of your screen, click the yellow "𝗥𝘂𝗻 𝗣𝗶𝗽𝗲𝗹𝗶𝗻𝗲" button. The app will shoot your data through the arrows, executing each node step-by-step until it reaches the end and calculates your Accuracy score.
  • 𝗧𝗿𝗼𝘂𝗯𝗹𝗲𝘀𝗵𝗼𝗼𝘁𝗶𝗻𝗴: What if you click "Run" and you get an 𝗘𝗿𝗿𝗼𝗿? . Check your arrows! Did you skip the Preprocessing node? Did you forget to connect the Model to the Evaluation node? Fixing broken arrows is how you learn to think like a true Data Scientist.
