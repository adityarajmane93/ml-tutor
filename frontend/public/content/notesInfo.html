The Learn & Notes System

Remember: If you ever feel stuck or frustrated, use the "How are you feeling?" tracker at the top of your screen to log your emotions.

1. The Learn Tab: Your Interactive Textbook

Machine Learning has a lot of complex vocabulary. The 𝗟𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗻 button at the top right of your screen is your dedicated AI tutor.

  • 𝗖𝗼𝗻𝘁𝗲𝘅𝘁-𝗔𝘄𝗮𝗿𝗲: Click on any node (like Dataset or Model) or any connection arrow on your canvas, then click the "Learn" button. The reading pane will automatically open to the exact chapter you need to understand that specific block.
  • 𝗔𝗰𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗥𝗲𝗮𝗱𝗶𝗻𝗴: Do not just read passively—interact! You can use your mouse to highlight important sentences or definitions in the text. This will automatically snap the text into the right-hand panel so you can take notes on it.

2. The Study Notes Panel

On the right side of the Learn Modal, you will see the Study Notes dashboard. This is where you document your computational thinking process.

  • 𝗖𝗮𝗽𝘁𝘂𝗿𝗲 𝗧𝗵𝗼𝘂𝗴𝗵𝘁𝘀: After highlighting a tricky concept, use the text box at the bottom of the panel to type out your own explanation or question.
  • 𝗦𝗮𝘃𝗲 & 𝗗𝗲𝗹𝗲𝘁𝗲: Click the blue "Save Note" button to lock it in. The note will be permanently tied to the exact time and place you made it. If you made a mistake, you can always delete it using the trash can (🗑) icon.

3. The "All Notes" Dashboard

What happens to your notes after you close the Learn modal? They are safely stored in your personal notebook!

  • 𝗔𝗰𝗰𝗲𝘀𝘀𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗡𝗼𝘁𝗲𝗯𝗼𝗼𝗸:On the main canvas, look at the floating toolbar on the far left. Click the yellow notebook icon (📒) to instantly open the "All Notes" view.
  • 𝗥𝗲𝘃𝗶𝗲𝘄 & 𝗝𝘂𝗺𝗽: Here, you can see every note you have taken across the entire activity, color-coded by the specific ML block they belong to.
  • 𝗧𝗶𝗺𝗲 𝗧𝗿𝗮𝘃𝗲𝗹: If you click on a saved note that contains a highlight, the app will instantly teleport you back to the exact paragraph you were reading when you wrote it!

📝 Summary Checklist:

  1. Try clicking a node on your canvas and opening the Learn Tab.
  2. Highlight a sentence you find interesting.
  3. Type a small note in the right panel and click "Save Note".
  4. Close the Learn Tab, open the All Notes dashboard (📒), and find your newly saved note!
